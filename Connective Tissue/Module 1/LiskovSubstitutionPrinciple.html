Child classes should never break the parent class' type definitions. Patkos Csaba, in SOLID Part 3

The Liskov Substitution Principle (LSP) purposes that derived classes should not modify the behavior of the classes/interfaces from which they are derived, such that the base class'/interface's output is free of tacit knowledge. In other words, the scope of responsibility of a given method is always the same, and may not be changed by derived (child) classes.
