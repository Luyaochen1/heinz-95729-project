A. High-level modules should not depend on low-level modules. Both should depend on abstractions.
B. Abstractions should not depend on details. Details should depend on abstractions. Robert C. Martin, in Agile Software Development: Principles, Patterns and Practices

In industry, you will also hear the Dependency Inversion Principle (DIP) referred to as, or in the context of Dependency Injection and Inversion of Control.

Loose coupling is desirable, whether we are writing small consumer apps, large enterprise apps, middle-ware or third-party toolsets. Loose coupling can be the difference between an organizations ability to maintain and improve their offering(s) or failing to keep pace with the market due to rigidity and lack of flexibility. It can (and should) be a deciding factor in client adoption.
