SOLID. Sounds good right? It sounds like a goal worth achieving; like something to strive for. It also sounds like something that can be measured, and if it can be measured, then there must be a formula one can apply to achieve it. I think that’s what Michael Feathers had in mind when he coined the acronym to help us remember the first five object-oriented principles of class design, collected and published by Robert C. Martin1.

At the core, these principles offer flexibility and maintainability. They help us write code that is easier to understand, at a micro-level.  Grasping a macro-understanding, a more wholesome view of our code, can become a challenge. We’ll introduce some practices, that should help you achieve that later.


SRP The Single Responsibility Principle
OCP The Open-Closed Principle
LSP The Liskov Substitution Principle
ISP The Interface Segregation Principle
DIP The Dependency Inversion Principle

1 Robert C. Martin published multiple books and articles discussing SOLID and other principles: http://www.objectmentor.com/PPP/
